What "Gifted" Looks Like in Georgia

The Georgia Department of Education (GaDOE) asks us to look for potential in four distinct areas. Our goal is to find students who demonstrate strength in these categories.

  • Mental Ability: How students think, reason, and solve novel problems.
  • Achievement: What students have learned and mastered in core subjects like reading and math.
  • Creativity: How students generate unique, original, and elaborate ideas.
  • Motivation: A student's drive, curiosity, and desire to take on a challenge.

A student doesn't have to be a top performer in all four areas. The state provides a couple of pathways for them to qualify.

The Path to Eligibility: Two Ways to Qualify

Eligibility is determined by meeting the score requirements on one of two state-approved pathways.

Pathway A

For students with exceptionally high scores in two specific areas.

  • A qualifying score in Mental Ability (99th percentile for K-2, 96th for grades 3+).
  • AND a qualifying score in Achievement (90th percentile or higher).

Pathway B

For students who show a combination of strengths across three different areas.

  • Qualifying scores in any three of the four categories (Mental Ability, Achievement, Creativity, or Motivation). The percentile cutoff is typically 90% or higher.

What Our Different Tools Measure

For Achievement (What they've learned):

  • MAP Test: Our universal screener for reading and math skills.
  • Iowa Assessment: Another nationally-normed test that measures academic skill, just with a different format and structure than the MAP.

For Mental Ability (How they think):

  • CogAT & Naglieri: These use puzzles and non-traditional problems to assess reasoning and problem-solving skills.

For Creativity (How they generate ideas):

  • Torrance Test: Measures creative thinking skills like originality and elaboration.

For Motivation (Their drive to learn):

  • Renzulli Scale: A rating scale where classroom teachers provide insight into a student's curiosity and persistence.

Understanding Your Child's Testing Path

If Your Child Scored 90% or Higher on MAP:

Congratulations! They have already earned a qualifying score in the Achievement category.

We invite them to test for Mental Ability (CogAT, Naglieri) and Creativity (Torrance) to see if they qualify through one of the state pathways.

If Your Child Scored 75-89% on MAP:

This indicates high potential!

We invite them for a broader look to see if they can meet the "Multiple-Criteria" path. They will test for Achievement (Iowa), Mental Ability (CogAT, Naglieri), and Creativity (Torrance).

Our district schedules these tests on Saturdays throughout the year to ensure every student has an opportunity.

Setting Your Child Up for Success

Preparing for Test Day:

  • Familiarization, Not Studying: Help your child feel confident by familiarizing them with the style of questions. Think puzzles, logic, and "out-of-the-box" thinking. The goal is comfort, not cramming.
  • Keep it Low-Pressure: The best message is "Have fun and try your best!" Frame it as a chance to tackle some interesting new challenges.
  • The Basics: A good night's sleep and a healthy breakfast are always key!
